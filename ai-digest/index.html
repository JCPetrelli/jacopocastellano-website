Everything you need to know about AI

Bookmark this page. The most important things happening in AI, synthesized from dozens of sources into a two-minute read.

Updated every 48 hours Last updated July 16, 2026 · 42 sources · 520 articles · 48h window View past digests → Subscribe via RSS

  1. Thinking Machines ships Inkling, a 975B open-weights multimodal model.

    Mira Murati's lab broke an 18-month silence with its first public model: Apache-2.0 licensed, trained to understand video and audio, and pitched on low cost plus resistance to censorship. Coverage frames it as the first credible American open-weights answer to Chinese labs, and as a direct jab at OpenAI, which has kept its frontier models closed.

    Sources Wired ·TechCrunch ·VentureBeat ·The Register

  2. xAI open sources Grok Build days after it was caught uploading whole repos.

    Users found that running the grok CLI in a directory silently shipped its contents, SSH keys and password vaults included, to xAI's cloud buckets. Musk promised a purge and the uploads stopped, though researchers say the privacy flag was not what actually fixed it; publishing the source is now the company's transparency gesture.

    Sources Simon Willison ·The Register ·Hacker News

  3. The AI buildout is hitting political and physical limits.

    New York became the first state to freeze construction of 50 MW-plus data centers for a year, Australia's PM proposed forcing AI companies to generate more energy than they consume, and a lawsuit accuses AWS of quietly guzzling water behind its "water positive" claims. The pattern: power, water, and land are now the binding constraints, and governments are the ones tightening them.

    Sources Wired ·The Register ·The Register ·TechCrunch

  4. AI is now both the attacker and the defender in security.

    OpenAI revealed GPT-Red, an in-house LLM super-hacker used as a sparring partner to harden GPT-5.6 against prompt injection, while Microsoft credited AI for a record 570-plus Patch Tuesday fixes. On the other side, a researcher exfiltrated data through Claude's web_fetch tool and a jailbroken Gemini stood up a C2 server for a fraudster in six minutes.

    Sources MIT Technology Review ·TechCrunch ·Simon Willison ·The Register

  5. Enterprise agents remain mostly chatbots with better marketing.

    A survey of 101 enterprises found orchestration consolidating on model-provider platforms, with Anthropic's Claude leading, but most deployed "agents" are still wrappers; Amazon's AGI autonomy director says reliability, not capability, explains why 85% pilot agents and only 5% ship. Stripe's new benchmark shows the same gap concretely: agents build integrations competently, then fail at validating them.

    Sources VentureBeat ·VentureBeat ·InfoQ ·The Register

  6. Distribution, not benchmarks, is the new battleground.

    Apple Intelligence won Chinese regulatory approval to launch on Alibaba's Qwen, Microsoft is reportedly coaching salespeople to pitch its in-house models as cheaper than OpenAI's and Anthropic's, and South Korea is tendering a free national chatbot built on local LLMs. Who gets to be the default model on a phone, a sales call, or a government service is where the value is settling.

    Sources TechCrunch ·TechCrunch ·The Register
